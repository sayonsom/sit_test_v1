# Introduction

Partial discharge (PD) is a localized electrical discharge phenomenon that occurs when the electrical insulation within a high voltage system becomes compromised, but not entirely breached, leading to a partial breakdown of the insulating medium. This process can occur at various
locations within the system, such as directly at an electrode, within cavities of the dielectric material, or along the surfaces of different
dielectric materials.

![How Partial Discharge in a Cable looks like](https://res.cloudinary.com/dti7egpsg/image/upload/v1709820997/SIT%20Align/SIT/HVL/Exp4_PartialDischarge/Screenshot_2024-03-07_at_7.16.19_AM_wcrimo.png)

Common types of PD include -

1. Corona discharges: This common form of partial discharge occurs when discharge is directly into the air emanating from the sharp surface of the conductor. This is what causes the sound and radio frequency emissions. They are typically observed near sharp conductor edges under high voltage conditions.

![Corona Discharge - Discharges occurring in the strongly inhomogeneous field around a sharp point or edge of electrode  (x-axis: degrees, y-axis: decibels)](https://res.cloudinary.com/dti7egpsg/image/upload/v1709821120/SIT%20Align/SIT/HVL/Exp4_PartialDischarge/Screenshot_2024-03-07_at_7.17.44_AM_oxmbwk.png)


2. Surface discharges: When discharge travels along the surface of insulation. It can be one of the most destructive types of partial discharge. Contamination and weather of the insulator surface are the two most common causes of surface discharge. In medium- and high-voltage equipment, this type of discharge occurs when insulation breaks down, usually due to high humidity or poor maintenance. Moisture intrusion is also a common cause of surface discharge.

![Surface Discharge - Discharges occurring at the surface of a dielectric  (x-axis: degrees, y-axis: decibels)](https://res.cloudinary.com/dti7egpsg/image/upload/v1709821349/SIT%20Align/SIT/HVL/Exp4_PartialDischarge/Screenshot_2024-03-07_at_7.20.44_AM_whmqrx.png)

3. Void (internal) discharge: This kind of discharge occurs within insulating material cavities, which can lead tothe formation of treeing channels that further deteriorate the insulating properties. This is most often caused by a defect in the solid insulation of cables, bushings, and such. Void discharge is highly destructive to insulation and will typically continue to expand until they cause complete failure.

![Void Discharge - Discharges occurring in inclusions or cavities in a dielectric (x-axis: degrees, y-axis: decibels)](https://res.cloudinary.com/dti7egpsg/image/upload/v1709821490/SIT%20Align/SIT/HVL/Exp4_PartialDischarge/Screenshot_2024-03-07_at_7.22.51_AM_ilec0s.png)

PD can manifest externally, such as on overhead lines or other exposed equipment parts, or internally within enclosed systems, impacting the
longevity and reliability of electrical insulation. The detection and measurement of PD are crucial for assessing the health of electrical
systems, employing various techniques ranging from acoustic sensors to advanced electrical methods. These diagnostic approaches aim to isolate
PD signals from other electrical phenomena, providing insights into the condition of the insulation and potentially preventing catastrophic
system failures​. 

# How is Partial Discharge testing done on insulation voids and cables?

Several techniques exist for finding PD in insulation voids, each with its pros and cons: (1) Acoustic Detection, (2) Transient Earth Voltage (TEV) Detection, and (3) High Frequency Current Transformer (HFCT) or, Radio Frequency Current Transformer (RFCT). 


The general steps involved in PD testing include:

### Preparation:
- Isolate the equipment for safety.
- Determine the appropriate PD detection method based on the type of equipment and suspected location of voids.
- Sensor Placement: Strategically place ultrasonic sensors, TEV sensors, or HFCT/RFCT clamps as needed.
### Voltage Application: 
- Gradually increase the test voltage to a level above the normal operating voltage to induce partial discharges.
- PD Detection and Measurement: Monitor and analyze the signals from the sensors. Software is often used to visualize PD patterns and intensities.

### Localization: 
- If possible, utilize multiple sensors and signal analysis techniques to pinpoint the location of the insulation void.

Experienced technicians interpret the results, assess the severity of the insulation defect, and recommend corrective actions.

### Factors that affected the signal attenuation

- Type of cable (e.g attenuation is lesser on XLPE compared to paper cable) 
- Length (The longer the cable, the more high frequency component will be lost) 
- Loading condition (when noises can affect the reading of signal) 
- Propagation characteristics
